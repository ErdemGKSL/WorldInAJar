[SIZE=5][COLOR=#AA55FF][B]Turn glass into an adventure[/B][/COLOR][/SIZE]

World In A Jar lets players craft glass jars that contain real, persistent, enterable miniature worlds. Place a jar in the outside world and its interior appears inside it as a live miniature scene. Step through its portal to build, survive, store items, and share the space at full size.

[SIZE=5][COLOR=#55FFFF][B]Highlights[/B][/COLOR][/SIZE]

[LIST]
[*][B]Persistent jar worlds[/B] — every jar keeps its identity, interior, occupants, name, scale, and location when placed, picked up, dropped, or stored.
[*][B]Two-way live previews[/B] — players outside see the miniature interior; players inside see the surrounding outside world.
[*][B]Enterable portals[/B] — players, mobs, items, and projectiles can cross between the jar and the real world.
[*][B]Portable worlds[/B] — pick up a jar without wiping what was built inside. Shift-right-click the item in your inventory to inspect its world.
[*][B]Modular construction[/B] — sneak-right-click a placed jar with another jar to attach their spaces. Sneak-break one glass cell to detach it again.
[*][B]Jar recall[/B] — after continuously holding a jar for the configured time, use [B]/jar back[/B] to recover it later.
[*][B]Named worlds[/B] — rename a jar in an anvil; its name persists in menus, commands, and future item drops.
[*][B]Isolated interiors[/B] — optional teleport and respawn protection prevents other mechanics or plugins from bypassing jar boundaries.
[*][B]Performance controls[/B] — previews, chunk snapshots, entity counts, and large interior edits are capped or spread across ticks.
[/LIST]

[SIZE=5][COLOR=#AA55FF][B]Designed for survival play[/B][/COLOR][/SIZE]

The jar recipe uses seven glass blocks:

[B]Glass — Air — Glass[/B]
[B]Glass — Air — Glass[/B]
[B]Glass — Glass — Glass[/B]

The removable [B]Jar Portal Side[/B] uses a 2×2 square of glass panes. Place the jar, right-click its portal face to enter, and right-click the matching interior wall to leave. A blocked portal really is blocked, so doorway placement matters.
